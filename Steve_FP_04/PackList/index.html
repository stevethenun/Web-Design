Here is the "Must Have" packing list!

Clothing:

  • Lightweight, quick-drying clothing (T-shirts, shorts, pants, skirts)
  • Long-sleeved shirts and pants
  • Swimwear
  • Rain gear (waterproof jacket and pants)
  • Sweaters or fleece jackets
  • Hat (wide-brimmed hat for sun protection)
  • Socks and underwear
  • Footwear (boat shoes, sandals, sneakers)

Personal Items:

  • Sunscreen: Broad-spectrum SPF 30 or higher
  • Insect repellent: Especially important for areas with mosquitoes and bugs
  • Sunglasses: Polarized sunglasses for glare reduction
  • Prescription medications: Sufficient supply for the duration of the trip
  • First aid kit: Including seasickness medication, bandages, antiseptic, etc.
  • Toiletries: Toothbrush, toothpaste, shampoo, conditioner, soap, etc.
  • Personal hygiene items: Feminine hygiene products, razors, etc.
  • Towels: Quick-dry towels are ideal for limited space

Additional Items:

  • Paddle Boards
  • Kayaks
  • Spare parts for your dingy
  • Extra tools in a toolbox

Entertainment and Miscellaneous:

  • Books, magazines, or e-reader: For leisure reading during downtime
  • Fishing gear: If you plan to fish along the route
  • Binoculars: For wildlife spotting and navigation
  • Camera: Capture memories of your journey
  • Portable power bank: Keep electronic devices charged
  • Navigational charts and guidebooks: Essential for route planning and information about stops along the way

Documents:

  • Boat documentation: Registration, insurance, and any required permits
  • Identification: Driver's license, passport, and any other relevant identification
  • Navigation documents: Charts, guides, and navigation notes
